Sorshe Hilish Recipe

Ingredients

For the Fish:

  • 6 pieces of Hilsa fish (about 500g)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons mustard oil

For the Mustard Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons mustard seeds
  • 1 tablespoon poppy seeds
  • 2-3 green chilies
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons mustard oil
  • 1/2 cup water

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Fish: Rub the Hilsa fish pieces with turmeric powder and salt. Set aside for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Mustard Paste: Soak the mustard seeds and poppy seeds in warm water for 10-15 minutes. Drain and grind them with the green chilies to a smooth paste. Add a little water if needed.
  3. Cook the Fish: Heat mustard oil in a pan until it starts to smoke. Reduce the heat and carefully add the marinated fish pieces. Fry them lightly on both sides until they are slightly golden. Remove and set aside.
  4. Prepare the Sauce: In the same pan, add the prepared mustard paste, turmeric powder, and salt. Cook for a few minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Simmer the Fish: Add 1/2 cup of water to the pan and bring it to a boil. Gently place the fried fish pieces in the mustard sauce. Cover and simmer on low heat for about 10-15 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through and the sauce thickens.
  6. Finish and Serve: Drizzle a little mustard oil on top before serving. Serve the Sorshe Hilish hot with steamed rice.

Tips

  • Fresh Fish: Use fresh Hilsa fish for the best flavor.
  • Grinding the Paste: Grind the mustard seeds and poppy seeds to a smooth paste to avoid bitterness.
  • Adjust Spice: Add more green chilies if you prefer a spicier dish.
  • Cooking Oil: Mustard oil adds an authentic flavor to the dish, but you can use vegetable oil if preferred.
