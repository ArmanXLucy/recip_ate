Szechuan Chicken Recipe

Ingredients

For the Chicken:

  • 1 pound chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

For the Szechuan Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chili paste or sauce (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons water (for thickening)

For Stir-Frying:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1/2 cup sliced green onions
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts or cashews

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, combine chicken pieces with cornstarch and soy sauce. Mix well and let it marinate for 15 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Sauce: In a small bowl, mix soy sauce, rice vinegar, hoisin sauce, chili paste, sugar, and cornstarch mixture. Set aside.
  3. Cook the Chicken: Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the marinated chicken and cook until browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes. Remove chicken from the skillet and set aside.
  4. Stir-Fry the Vegetables: In the same skillet, add the remaining tablespoon of vegetable oil. Add minced garlic, ginger, and sliced bell peppers. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  5. Add the Sauce: Return the cooked chicken to the skillet. Pour in the prepared Szechuan sauce and stir well to combine. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, until the sauce thickens and coats the chicken.
  6. Finish and Serve: Stir in the sliced green onions and roasted peanuts or cashews. Serve hot with steamed rice or noodles.

Tips

  • Adjust the Heat: If you prefer spicier dishes, add more chili paste or include fresh chopped chili peppers.
  • Vegetable Variations: You can add other vegetables such as broccoli, snap peas, or carrots to enhance the dish.
  • Protein Options: Substitute chicken with beef, pork, or tofu for different variations of the recipe.
