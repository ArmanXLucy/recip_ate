Biryani Recipe

Ingredients

For the Rice:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste

For the Chicken Marinade:

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • Salt to taste

For the Biryani:

  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 4-5 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1/2 cup fried onions (for garnish)
  • Saffron strands soaked in 1/4 cup warm milk
  • 1/4 cup ghee (clarified butter)

Instructions

  1. Cook the Rice: Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add the bay leaves, green cardamom pods, cloves, cinnamon stick, and salt. Add the rice and cook until it is 70% cooked. Drain the rice and set aside.
  2. Marinate the Chicken: In a large bowl, mix the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala powder, and salt. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour.
  3. Prepare the Biryani Base: Heat vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and cook until golden brown. Add the tomatoes and cook until they soften. Add the marinated chicken and cook until the chicken is cooked through.
  4. Layer the Biryani: In the pot with the cooked chicken, spread a layer of cooked rice. Sprinkle half of the chopped mint leaves, cilantro, and fried onions. Repeat with another layer of rice, followed by the remaining mint leaves, cilantro, and fried onions. Drizzle the saffron milk and ghee over the top.
  5. Dum Cooking: Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. You can seal the edges with dough to trap the steam. Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  6. Serve: Gently fluff the rice with a fork, making sure not to break the grains. Serve hot with raita or a side salad.

Tips

  • Marinate for Flavor: Marinating the chicken for a longer time enhances the flavor.
  • Use Basmati Rice: Basmati rice gives the best texture and flavor for biryani.
  • Even Layering: Ensure even layers of rice and chicken for balanced flavor distribution.
  • Dum Cooking: Dum cooking is essential for the authentic biryani experience. Make sure to seal the pot properly.
