Butter Cauliflower and Chickpeas Recipe

Ingredients

For the Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 1 can (15 oz) tomato sauce
  • 1 cup heavy cream or coconut milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Cauliflower and Chickpeas:

  • 1 large cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  • Cooked rice or naan, for serving

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Prepare the Cauliflower and Chickpeas: In a large bowl, toss the cauliflower florets and chickpeas with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread evenly on a baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes, or until the cauliflower is tender and golden.
  3. Make the Sauce: While the vegetables are roasting, heat the butter and olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the garlic and ginger, cooking for another 2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add the Spices: Stir in the garam masala, cumin, turmeric, coriander, and cayenne pepper. Cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly, until the spices are toasted and fragrant.
  5. Add the Tomato Sauce and Cream: Pour in the tomato sauce and stir to combine. Simmer for 5 minutes, then stir in the heavy cream or coconut milk. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Combine and Serve: Add the roasted cauliflower and chickpeas to the sauce, stirring to coat. Simmer for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Serve over cooked rice or with naan, garnished with fresh cilantro.

Tips

  • Roasting for Flavor: Roasting the cauliflower and chickpeas adds a nice texture and enhances the flavor. Make sure they are spread out evenly on the baking sheet to get crispy edges.
  • Spice Level: Adjust the amount of cayenne pepper to suit your spice preference. You can also add a splash of lemon juice for a bit of tang.
  • Make it Vegan: Use coconut milk instead of heavy cream and replace the butter with more olive oil or a vegan butter substitute.
  • Serving Suggestions: This dish is perfect served with basmati rice, naan, or even over quinoa. It's also great with a side of steamed vegetables or a fresh salad.
