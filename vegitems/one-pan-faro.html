One-Pan Farro with Tomatoes and Kale Recipe

Ingredients

For the Dish:

  • 1 cup farro, rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 bunch kale, stems removed and leaves chopped
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large skillet or sauté pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the garlic and red pepper flakes (if using), and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  2. Add the Tomatoes: Stir in the cherry tomatoes and a pinch of salt. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the tomatoes begin to soften and release their juices, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the Farro and Broth: Add the rinsed farro to the pan, along with the vegetable broth or water. Stir to combine. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer. Cover and cook for 20-25 minutes, or until the farro is tender and the liquid is mostly absorbed.
  4. Add the Kale: Stir in the chopped kale and cook for an additional 5 minutes, or until the kale is wilted and tender.
  5. Season and Serve: Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot, topped with grated Parmesan cheese if desired.

Tips

  • Farro Cooking: Depending on the type of farro you use (pearled, semi-pearled, or whole), cooking times may vary. Pearled farro cooks faster, while whole farro may require additional water and time.
  • Customizing Vegetables: Feel free to add other vegetables like bell peppers, zucchini, or spinach. Adjust cooking times accordingly.
  • Making it Vegan: Skip the Parmesan cheese or use a dairy-free alternative for a vegan version.
  • Storage: Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat gently on the stovetop or in the microwave.
