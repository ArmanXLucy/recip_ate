Steph’s Chickpea Curry Recipe

Ingredients

For the Curry:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder (optional, for heat)
  • 2 cans (15 oz each) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 cup vegetable broth or water
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup spinach or kale, chopped (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Cooked rice or naan, for serving

Instructions

  1. Heat the Oil: In a large pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add Aromatics: Add the garlic and ginger, and cook for another 1-2 minutes, until fragrant.
  3. Spices: Stir in the curry powder, cumin, coriander, turmeric, and chili powder (if using). Cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly, to toast the spices.
  4. Add Chickpeas and Tomatoes: Add the chickpeas and diced tomatoes to the pan. Stir to combine.
  5. Add Liquid: Pour in the coconut milk and vegetable broth (or water). Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook for 20-25 minutes, until the sauce has thickened slightly and the flavors have melded.
  6. Add Greens: If using spinach or kale, stir it into the curry and cook for another 2-3 minutes, until wilted.
  7. Season and Serve: Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve the curry over cooked rice or with naan, garnished with fresh cilantro.

Tips

  • Customize the Heat: Adjust the chili powder to your taste. For a milder curry, omit the chili powder; for more heat, add more or include fresh chilies.
  • Add More Veggies: You can add other vegetables like bell peppers, carrots, or peas to the curry for added nutrition and flavor.
  • Make it Creamier: For an even creamier curry, add an extra half can of coconut milk or a splash of cream.
  • Leftovers: This curry tastes even better the next day as the flavors meld. Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
