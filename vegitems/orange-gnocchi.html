Cauliflower Orange Gnocchi Recipe

Ingredients

For the Gnocchi:

  • 1 large head cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (plus more for dusting)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Zest of 1 orange

For the Orange Butter Sauce:

  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • Juice of 1 orange
  • 1 tablespoon fresh sage, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese, for serving

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Cauliflower: Steam or boil the cauliflower florets until they are very tender. Drain well and allow to cool slightly. Use a food processor or blender to puree the cauliflower until smooth.
  2. Make the Gnocchi Dough: In a large bowl, combine the cauliflower puree, flour, salt, pepper, and orange zest. Mix until a dough forms. Add more flour if the dough is too sticky.
  3. Shape the Gnocchi: Divide the dough into sections and roll each section into a long rope about 1/2 inch in diameter. Cut the rope into 1-inch pieces. Use a fork to press gently on each piece to create ridges, if desired.
  4. Cook the Gnocchi: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the gnocchi in batches, cooking until they float to the surface (about 2-3 minutes). Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside.
  5. Make the Orange Butter Sauce: In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the orange zest, orange juice, and chopped sage. Cook for a few minutes until the sauce is fragrant and slightly reduced. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Toss the Gnocchi: Add the cooked gnocchi to the skillet with the orange butter sauce. Toss to coat the gnocchi evenly in the sauce.
  7. Serve: Serve the gnocchi hot, topped with grated Parmesan cheese and additional sage if desired.

Tips

  • Flour Amount: The amount of flour needed may vary depending on the moisture content of the cauliflower. Add more flour as needed to form a workable dough.
  • Cooking Gnocchi: Be sure to cook the gnocchi in batches to avoid overcrowding the pot, which can cause them to stick together.
  • Flavor Variations: For a different twist, try adding other herbs like thyme or rosemary to the sauce. You can also sprinkle toasted nuts on top for added crunch.
  • Storing Leftovers: Store any leftover gnocchi in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat gently in a skillet with a little butter or oil.
